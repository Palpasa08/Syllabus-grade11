1. Nepal's economy to grow by 3.9 percent this fiscal year: World Bank
The World Bank has projected Nepal's economy to grow by 3.9 percent in the current fiscal year 2023-24, up from an estimated 1.9 percent in the last fiscal year.

2. Nepal's trade deficit narrows by 4.6 percent in first eight months of current fiscal year
Nepal's trade deficit narrowed by 4.6 percent to Rs816.78 billion in the first eight months of the current fiscal year compared to the same period last fiscal year.

Nepal suffered a 35-run defeat against the United Arab Emirates in their opening match of the ACC Premier Cup at the Al Amerat Cricket Ground in Oman on Friday.

2. Nepali climbers scale Mt Annapurna I
A team of Nepali climbers has successfully scaled Mt Annapurna I (8,091m), the 10th highest mountain in the world, on Saturday morning.

The Nepali film 'Shambala' has been selected for the prestigious Tribeca Film Festival in New York, becoming the first Nepali film to be featured in the festival's Viewpoints section.

The 21st edition of the Kathmandu International Mountain Film Festival has announced a call for entries for films made between January 2022 and May 2024.
